As the Sky Cracks, We Are Not Alone

by Ananya Sharma

“The sky is falling, but my hand is in yours.
If this is the end, then let’s meet it together.”
— A note found taped to a streetlamp in Vienna.

Global Collapse Confirmed.

Over 70% of Earth’s infrastructure has failed in the past 48 hours. Entire regions have vanished from contact — power grids down, communications severed, transport systems crumbled. Governments are silent. Emergency broadcasts have gone dark mid-transmission.

Reports suggest a series of cascading breakdowns: tectonic surges, geomagnetic instability, and unexplained atmospheric interference.

Some cities — entire countries — have disappeared from satellite feeds in minutes. The ground shakes in waves. The skies are turning strange. And still, no one seems to know exactly why.

World Leaders Respond — Carefully.

Despite the scale of devastation, world leaders continue to issue statements of calm.

“We are in control,” said Prime Minister Kavita Rao in a late-evening address. “We are activating all defense protocols and global alliances. We ask you to stay indoors, stay with your loved ones, and await further instruction.”

No explanations. No timelines. Just the quiet insistence that everything will be fine.

Unofficially, governments across remaining networks are implementing shelter-in-place mandates. Civilians are advised to avoid sharing unverified media. Supply chains are suspended indefinitely.

But All Across the World, a Different Story Is Unfolding.

With no signals, no orders, and no distractions, communities are coming together.

In the ruins of power grids, people are lighting candles. On rooftops in Tokyo, families are singing old lullabies. In Lisbon, children are chalking messages into pavement: We were here. We are still here.

In the absence of answers, there is something else: presence.

Even as the skies darken and the timeline nears its end, people are choosing to say goodbye together. Not in panic. Not in isolation. But in love.

And if this truly is the end — let the world remember us as we were in this moment:

Not alone. Not lost. Standing together, hand in hand.
